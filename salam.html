Salam is a contract in which the buyer pays the full purchase price in advance, while the goods are delivered at a future date. It is commonly used in agricultural financing to provide farmers with working capital before the harvest.

  • Widely used in agricultural financing
  • Advance payment with future delivery
  • Based on real trade ‚Äî not interest

Why Banks Use Salam Today

Although Salam came from the agricultural economy of early Islamic history, it fits surprisingly well in modern Islamic banking. Many Islamic banks in Pakistan and around the world use Salam to:

  • Fund agricultural production, such as wheat, cotton, or sugarcane
  • Support manufacturers who need capital before production
  • Facilitate supply chain financing for businesses
  • Provide Shariah compliant liquidity to farmers and SMEs

How It Works
