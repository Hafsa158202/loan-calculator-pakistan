Ijara (Islamic Leasing)

A Shariah-Compliant Alternative to Conventional Leasing

üè¶ What is Ijara?

Ijara is an Islamic financing concept where the bank purchases an asset (such as a home, car, or machinery) and leases it to the customer for a fixed period. Ownership stays with the bank, while the customer uses the asset in return for periodic rental payments. This model avoids interest (riba) and follows Shariah principles.

  • Used for car, home, or equipment financing
  • Ownership remains with the bank, usage with the customer
  • Shariah-compliant and interest-free leasing model

üìÑ How Does Ijara Work?

  1. The customer selects the asset they wish to lease (e.g., car, house, or machinery).
  2. The bank purchases the asset and retains ownership.
  3. The customer signs a lease agreement with fixed tenure and rent.
  4. Customer makes regular rental payments (not interest-based).
  5. After the lease term, the bank may transfer ownership (Ijara-wa-Iqtina model).
