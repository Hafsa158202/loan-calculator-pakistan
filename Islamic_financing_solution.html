Understanding Murabaha and Musharakah in Islamic Banking

Islamic banking has become one of the fastest growing sectors in global finance. Unlike conventional banking, it operates on principles of fairness, transparency, and social justice, strictly avoiding interest (riba) and unethical investment practices. The foundation of Islamic finance is built on real trade, asset-backed transactions, and risk-sharing. Among the many Shariah compliant financing methods, Murabaha and Musharakah stand out as two of the most commonly used and trusted modes. Both aim to promote halal finance and create a balanced financial system that supports real economic growth without exploitation.

Murabaha Financing

Cost-plus financing where the bank buys an asset and sells it at an agreed markup in installments.

Diminishing Musharakah

Bank and customer jointly own property; customer gradually buys bank’s share while paying rent.
